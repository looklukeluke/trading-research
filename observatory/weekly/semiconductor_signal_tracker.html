📐 半导体投资框架入门 Semiconductor Investment Framework (互联网人视角 — for software/internet professionals)

理解半导体供应链的4层结构，是读懂本页所有信号的前提。Understanding the 4-layer supply chain is prerequisite to reading all signals on this page.

1
设备层 Equipment — ASML, AMAT — 给工厂造机器 = "卖铲子的人" They make the machines that make the chips. Like selling shovels in a gold rush. ASML is the ONLY EUV lithography supplier on Earth.
2
制造层 Foundry — TSMC (垄断) — 按设计图造芯片 = "工厂" TSMC manufactures chips for EVERYONE (NVIDIA, AMD, Apple, Google). They are the only factory capable of making cutting-edge (<5nm) chips. This is why "TSMC always wins" regardless of who designs the chip.
3
设计层 Design — NVDA, AMD, AVGO — 画设计图 = "建筑师" They design chips but don't make them. NVIDIA designs GPUs, AMD designs competing GPUs, Broadcom designs custom ASICs for Google/Amazon. The "CUDA moat" debate is about whether NVIDIA's software lock-in protects their design dominance.
4
需求层 Demand — MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN — 买芯片用 = "业主" They BUY chips and deploy AI. Their CapEx decisions ($602B in 2026) drive the entire chain. If they stop spending, everything collapses. This layer has 0% coverage in our research — the biggest blind spot.
核心投资逻辑 Core Logic: 不管谁的设计赢（NVIDIA还是AMD还是Google自研），都需要TSMC制造 + ASML的机器。这就是Tier 1 "基础设施永远赢" 的核心理由。 Regardless of who wins the design war, TSMC manufactures ALL of them, and ASML provides ALL the machines. This is why Tier 1 = "infrastructure always wins."

§I 月度追踪信号Monthly Signals

四个月度信号覆盖：出货量（TSMC）、定价（DRAM）、竞争（GPU ASP）、政策（出口管制）。这是最快的预警层。

如何读TSMC月度数据 How to Read TSMC Monthly

关注YoY增速而非绝对值。AI占TSMC收入约50%，当总营收YoY增速从40%降到20%以下，说明AI芯片出货增速在减速。注意：季节性因素（Q1通常最弱）需要用同比而非环比消除。

数据来源：investor.tsmc.com — Monthly Revenue页面，每月10日更新。

§II 季度追踪信号Quarterly Signals

七个季度信号覆盖整条供应链：设计（NVDA/AMD） → 代工（TSMC） → 设备（ASML） → 存储（Hynix/Micron） → 基础设施（AVGO） → 买家（Hyperscalers）。每个财报季是一次"全链条体检"。

季报阅读优先级 Earnings Reading Priority
  1. NVIDIA（核心验证） → 先看指引和毛利率，再看数据中心收入拆分
  2. Hyperscaler CapEx（需求验证） → 只看CapEx数字和management commentary on AI spend
  3. TSMC（供应验证） → CoWoS利用率和先进制程revenue mix
  4. AMD / AVGO（竞争验证） → MI系列出货和ASIC AI收入
  5. ASML / Memory（长期验证） → 订单趋势和HBM pricing

§III 年度追踪信号Annual / Semi-annual Signals

结构性信号变化缓慢但影响深远。每6-12个月做一次深度评估。关注"方向变化"而非"绝对值"。

结构性信号评估方法 Structural Signal Method

结构性信号不看"绝对值"而看"方向变化"。例如：RISC-V在数据中心从0.5%到1%不重要，但从1%到5%的加速曲线很重要 — 这意味着ARM的PE倍数基础在动摇。

每6个月对6个基线判断做一次全面交叉验证，用最新的结构性信号更新概率。

META Strength: 6-Node Integration ▾

Unique integration depth: This is the only tracking system that synthesizes 8 supply chain nodes (GPU design, lithography, foundry, HBM, custom silicon, EDA/IP, demand side, AI lab) with 20 cognitive agents into a single forward-looking signal framework. Each signal is cross-referenced against multiple nodes rather than tracked in isolation. When TSMC monthly revenue spikes, this tracker connects it to Node 1 (GPU demand), Node 3 (foundry utilization), and Node 4 (HBM packaging demand) simultaneously.

From research to action: The V1 tracker had signals and probabilities but no explicit action mapping. V2 closes the loop: every signal now has a specific investment action (HOLD/ADD/REDUCE/SELL) and ticker links to the Trading Dashboard. The chain is: Signal detected → Probability updated → Tier action triggered → Dashboard execution. This is the difference between “interesting information” and “actionable intelligence.”

Temporal layering: The 4-layer frequency structure (monthly/quarterly/semi-annual/annual) prevents two failure modes: (1) overreacting to noise (monthly data fluctuations) and (2) missing structural shifts (annual technology transitions). Each layer has different adjustment rules — L1 signals get immediate ±10-20% probability updates, while L3 signals accumulate into quarterly reviews. This mirrors how professional macro funds structure their signal processing: fast signals for tactical, slow signals for strategic.

META Risk: Supply-side Echo Chamber ▾

Systematic bias update (2026-03-21): Original 16 agents were all supply-side. Agents #17-20 (Altman, Nadella, Pichai, Hassabis) plus Musk (#12, cross-listed) now add demand-side coverage. Ratio improved to 15 supply : 5 demand. But depth asymmetry remains — supply-side has 3+ specialized source files per agent vs demand-side just established. Treat supply-side-only consensus with continued +5-10% uncertainty band on bearish scenarios.

What this means concretely: When Jensen says “demand is insatiable” and TSMC confirms “we cannot build CoWoS fast enough,” this looks like independent confirmation. But it is actually two sides of the same coin — the seller saying “everyone wants to buy” and the manufacturer saying “we cannot make enough.” Neither tells you whether the end-user (the company deploying AI in production) is actually seeing ROI. The demand-side question — “Is $602B in CapEx generating proportional revenue for the buyers?” — remains unanswered.

Probability implication: The “AI CapEx bubble” probability (15-20%) may be systematically underestimated because our entire cognitive agent network has an incentive to believe the spend is rational. A demand-side agent (e.g., a CTO of a Fortune 500 company trying to deploy AI) might rate this risk at 25-35%. Until demand-side agents are added, treat all probabilities with a +5-10% uncertainty band on bearish scenarios.

META Automation Gap ▾

Semi-automatable signals: Monthly quantitative data (TSMC revenue, DRAM contract prices, GPU ASP) can be semi-automatically scraped. TSMC publishes monthly revenue in a consistent format on investor.tsmc.com. TrendForce publishes contract price reports. Mercury Research publishes GPU market share. A simple Python pipeline could fetch these on the 10th of each month and update the Latest Signals panel automatically.

Human-judgment-only signals: But the most valuable signals are qualitative and resist automation. Jensen’s tone shift at GTC (“we used to say unprecedented demand, now he says sustainable demand — is that confidence or hedging?”). Lisa Su’s body language when discussing MI400 timeline. The gap between what a CEO says in the earnings call vs. what they do in the next quarter. These require human pattern-matching against years of accumulated context about each person’s communication style.

Current state: fully manual. As of March 2026, every signal in this tracker is manually updated. There is no automated data pipeline, no alert system, no dashboard integration. The risk: signals are missed or delayed because updating requires remembering to check 6+ sources on specific dates. The recommended first step is not full automation but a simple calendar reminder system: TSMC revenue day (10th), TrendForce report (mid-month), earnings dates (flagged 1 week ahead). Even this minimal discipline would significantly reduce missed signals.

META ADHD Warning: Tracking != Trading ▾

TRAP Action substitution risk. Building a comprehensive signal tracking system is deeply satisfying work — categorizing signals, designing probability frameworks, creating tier structures. It activates the same “productivity” circuits as actual trading research. But tracking signals and executing trades are fundamentally different activities. The P0 sell-side research episode (5 days of research, zero trades executed) is the template for how this can go wrong.

The honest question: In the 3 weeks since the 20 cognitive agent reports were completed, how many actual trades were executed based on their insights? If the answer is zero, then Signal Tracker is functioning as an elaborate procrastination device — creating the feeling of “working on trading” without the discomfort of making actual buy/sell decisions under uncertainty.

Antidote: outcome tracking. This tracker’s value can only be validated by tracking a specific metric: “Signal detected → Action recommended → Trade executed → P&L outcome.” If after 3 months, the Signal → Trade conversion rate is below 20%, the system is providing intellectual stimulation rather than trading edge. At that point, the honest response is to simplify radically: reduce to 3 signals (TSMC monthly, NVDA quarterly, DRAM price) and force a trade decision on each one.

META Chain Break: Signal-to-Dashboard ▾

The intended chain: Signal Tracker (detect) → Judgment (evaluate probability) → Action (decide tier adjustment) → Trading Dashboard (execute) → Review (measure outcome). This is a clean five-step loop that, in theory, converts research insight into trading P&L. Each step feeds the next: detection triggers evaluation, evaluation triggers action, action triggers execution.

Where it breaks: The chain currently breaks between Action and Dashboard. The Signal Tracker recommends “HOLD NVDA” or “evaluate AMD for entry,” but the Trading Dashboard does not read the Signal Tracker’s output. There is no automated handoff. The Dashboard shows price charts and technical indicators, but it does not display “Signal Tracker says: 3 bull signals accumulated, consider increasing Tier 1 weight.” This means the trader must manually carry the Signal Tracker’s recommendation to the Dashboard — a step that is easily forgotten or deprioritized.

Potential fix: The simplest bridge would be a “Signal Summary” widget on the Trading Dashboard homepage that displays: (1) current signal count by direction (bull/bear/watch), (2) most recent L1/L2 signal, (3) current portfolio tier recommendations. This widget could read from a JSON file that the Signal Tracker exports on each update. The technology is trivial; the discipline of actually updating the JSON is the real bottleneck. This is why the Update Discipline note (m6) matters as much as the technical architecture.

META Update Discipline ▾

Recommended cadence: Every month on the 10th (TSMC revenue release day), do a complete signal scan. This is the natural anchor because TSMC revenue is the single most important leading indicator. The scan should take 30-60 minutes: check TSMC number, check DRAM prices, check any ad-hoc news (export controls, M&A, product launches), update the Latest Signals panel, update any affected baseline probabilities.

Quarterly deep review: During each earnings season (February, May, August, November), do a full baseline probability review. This is when all 7 quarterly signals fire simultaneously. Block 2-3 hours after NVIDIA reports (the most important single earnings) to: update all 6 baseline probabilities, review tier allocations, check milestone calendar for upcoming catalysts, update the right sidebar notes if any meta-assessments have changed.

L1 emergency protocol: When a Level 1 signal fires (NVDA miss >10%, CapEx cut >20%, Taiwan crisis, DRAM crash >15%), evaluate within 24 hours. Do not wait for the monthly scan. L1 signals are rare (1-2 per year) but each one can move probabilities by 10-20 percentage points. The update should be: (1) update affected baseline probability, (2) check tier allocation implications, (3) record the signal and your assessment in the Latest Signals panel, (4) communicate any trade decision to the Trading Dashboard.

Decay risk: The biggest threat to this tracker is not inaccuracy but abandonment. A signal tracker that is updated for 2 months and then forgotten is worse than no tracker at all — because stale probabilities create false confidence. If you find yourself skipping 2+ monthly scans in a row, it is better to archive this page and rely on 3 simple alerts (TSMC monthly, NVDA quarterly, DRAM monthly) than to maintain a comprehensive-but-stale tracker.

结构性偏差：所有bearish概率可能被系统性低估5-10%。特别是"AI CapEx泡沫"(15-20%)和"CUDA护城河5年+"(35%) — 如果加入中国AI公司在Ascend上成功训练的证据，CUDA的不可替代性会进一步下降。

数据质量悖论：中国半导体数据最不透明的地方恰恰是最重要的地方 — SMIC良率、华为出货量、大基金实际拨付。这意味着我们在最需要精确判断的维度上信息最少。承认这个事实比用不可靠数据填充更诚实。

META 数据质量警告 ▾

TRAP 中国半导体数据的三层不透明：

Layer 1 — 公司层：SMIC财报只披露收入和产能利用率，不披露按制程节点的良率和出货。华为海思完全不公开Ascend出货数据。所有"出货量"数字来自TrendForce/供应链估计，误差带可能±30%。

Layer 2 — 政策层：大基金一期/二期已暴露严重腐败(多名高管被查)。三期¥3,440亿的实际拨付节奏和投向无法独立验证。"政府目标70%自给率"是政治口号还是工程路线图？

Layer 3 — 叙事层：西方分析师倾向高估管制效果("China can't do it")，中国媒体倾向高估自主进展("技术突破")。真相在中间，但中间在哪里没人知道。

操作建议：对中国相关概率，使用宽置信区间(±15%而非±5%)。宁可承认不确定，不要假装精确。

KNOW CUDA是什么？ ▾

类比：CUDA之于GPU = iOS之于iPhone。 CUDA是NVIDIA的GPU编程框架。过去15年，全球90%的AI研究代码都基于CUDA编写。开发者已经习惯CUDA，切换成本极高——就像从iOS转Android要重写所有App。

为什么置信度从80%降到35%：2024年Claude和Gemini在Google TPU上训练成功，证明前沿AI模型可以完全绕过CUDA。PyTorch 2.0的torch.compile()开始创建硬件无关的编译层——相当于"写一次代码，自动适配所有芯片"。

KNOW HBM是什么？ ▾

类比：HBM之于GPU = RAM之于CPU，但堆在旁边。 High Bandwidth Memory(高带宽内存)是堆叠在GPU芯片旁边的超快内存。AI训练需要同时处理海量数据，普通内存速度不够，HBM速度是普通DRAM的10倍。

为什么是supercycle：每块AI GPU需要6-8颗HBM。NVIDIA一年卖数百万块GPU → 数千万颗HBM需求。SK Hynix和Micron的产能全被预订到2027年。上一轮DRAM supercycle是2017年加密矿机热潮，持续18个月后DRAM价格暴跌60%。当前HBM已持续~24个月——超过上轮周期长度。

KNOW CoWoS封装是什么？ ▾

类比：CoWoS = 把GPU和HBM用胶水粘在一起的技术。 Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate是TSMC独家的先进封装技术。它把GPU芯片和HBM芯片并排放在同一块硅基板上，用微小的金属线连接。这是AI芯片的物理瓶颈——TSMC的CoWoS产能直接限制了全球AI GPU的产量。

数字感受：2024年CoWoS月产能约8万片 → 2026年目标20万片。但需求可能超过30万片。即使TSMC拼命扩产，供给缺口仍然存在——这就是为什么NVIDIA把CoWoS称为"最紧缺的资源"。

KNOW 为什么TSMC是垄断？ ▾

简单回答：地球上只有TSMC能做最先进的芯片。制造5nm以下芯片需要EUV光刻机(ASML独供) + 数百道工序的良率控制 + 数千亿美元的持续投资。Samsung尝试了10年，良率仍然不合格。Intel曾经领先，但2016年后落后整整2代。

投资含义：不管NVIDIA、AMD、Google谁赢，芯片都要在TSMC制造。TSM是"不选边的基础设施"。唯一风险是台海地缘政治——这是尾部风险（概率低但影响极大）。TSMC正在Arizona建厂缓解这个风险，但产能要到2028年才有意义。

KNOW EUV光刻是什么？ ▾

类比：EUV光刻 = 用极细的光束在芯片上"刻字"。Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography使用13.5nm波长的极紫外光，在芯片上刻出比病毒还小的电路图案。每台EUV光刻机重150吨、价格$3.5亿、需要3架747飞机运输。

ASML垄断：ASML是地球上唯一能制造EUV光刻机的公司。没有竞争对手，没有替代品。任何想制造先进芯片的公司(TSMC/Samsung/Intel)都必须排队买ASML的机器。Backlog(未交付订单)约€40B，提供2-3年收入可见性。这就是为什么ASML是"最稳固的垄断"。

KNOW $1T订单 = LOI不是合同 ▾

Jensen说"$1T订单可见性"到底什么意思？LOI = Letter of Intent(意向书)，是客户表示"我有意愿在未来几年买这么多芯片"的非约束性承诺。它不是合同——客户可以随时取消或减少。Colette Kress(NVDA CFO)承认OpenAI的$100B LOI "still at LOI stage"。

历史对比：LOI转化为实际合同的比率在不同行业差异很大：航空业~70-80%，科技行业~40-60%。如果按50%转化率算，$1T订单可能实际只有$500-600B——这仍然是巨大的，但比Jensen的叙事少了40%。

KNOW 毛利率73%意味什么？ ▾

NVDA毛利率73% = 芯片行业历史最高水平。对比：Intel毛利率~45%，AMD~50%，苹果~46%，高通~56%。NVDA能维持73%是因为CUDA生态锁定+供不应求+没有真正竞争者。

如果降到55%：收入不变的情况下，净利润减少约25%。$78B季度收入 × 73%毛利 = $57B毛利。降到55% = $43B毛利，少了$14B/季度。对股价的影响：PE倍数可能从25x → 20x（因为增长确定性降低），叠加利润下降 → 股价可能下跌30-40%。这就是为什么"毛利率压缩到55-65%"这个75%概率判断如此重要。

KNOW $602B AI CapEx规模感 ▾

$602B有多大？等于挪威一年GDP。等于全球每人花$75在AI基础设施上。MSFT/GOOG/AMZN/META四家2026年AI CapEx合计约$300B(占总额一半)，每家每天投入约$2亿。

历史对比：2023年AI CapEx约$200B → 2024年~$350B → 2026年$602B = 3年翻3倍。2000年电信泡沫时期，电信CapEx也是3年翻3倍($100B→$300B)，然后暴跌70%。当前最关键的差异：Hyperscaler们现金充裕(MSFT $75B+, GOOG $100B+, META $50B+)，且AI收入已经在增长(不像2000年电信收入几乎为零)。但相似之处令人不安。

KNOW ROCm = AMD的CUDA ▾

类比：ROCm之于CUDA = Android之于iOS。ROCm是AMD的GPU编程框架，理论上功能对标CUDA。问题在于生态——CUDA有15年的代码积累，ROCm只有5年，且兼容性和调试工具远不如CUDA成熟。

为什么MI400很关键：AMD MI400是第一款在硬件性能上可能匹配NVIDIA H200的芯片。但即使硬件够强，如果ROCm软件体验差（代码跑不通、调试困难），客户仍然不会切换。这就是"35% CUDA护城河"判断的核心：硬件在追赶，但软件生态差距可能缩小也可能长期存在。

KNOW RISC-V威胁 ▾

类比：RISC-V之于ARM = Linux之于Windows。RISC-V是开源芯片架构——任何公司都可以免费使用，不需要向ARM交授权费。ARM每颗芯片收取1-2%版税，RISC-V收取0%。

为什么是存亡级威胁：ARM的169x PE完全建立在"每颗移动/服务器芯片都付ARM版税"的预期上。如果RISC-V在数据中心份额超过5%，ARM的版税增速会被打断，169x PE将失去支撑。想想Linux用了20年从"玩具"变成服务器市场90%份额——RISC-V可能走同样的路，只是速度可能更快或更慢。当前RISC-V在数据中心份额<1%，但正在加速。

KNOW SMIC 7nm是怎么做到的？ ▾

多重曝光(Multi-patterning)：正常7nm用ASML的EUV光刻机一次曝光搞定。SMIC没有EUV，用老一代DUV(深紫外)光刻机对同一片晶圆曝光多次(4-7次)来达到更小的线宽。

类比：EUV=高清相机拍一张清晰照片。DUV多重曝光=用低清相机拍5-7张，后期叠合成一张高清 — 能做到，但慢、贵、废品率高。

代价：(1)良率低 — 每次曝光都有对齐误差累积，估计良率50-60%(TSMC EUV良率>90%) (2)产能极低 — 曝光次数多=生产速度慢，月产<5,000片 vs TSMC 3nm>10万片 (3)成本高 — 单颗芯片成本可能是TSMC的2-3倍

为什么重要：这证明中国在技术上能造出7nm芯片，但产能和成本限制意味着无法大规模替代进口。真正的分水岭是SMEE何时交付14nm DUV — 那将使中国在成熟制程上彻底摆脱ASML依赖。

KNOW Ascend 910C vs H100 ▾

Ascend 910C是华为海思的AI训练芯片，SMIC 7nm制造。对标NVIDIA H100。

性能对比(行业估计)：

  • FP16训练：~400 TFLOPS vs H100 ~990 TFLOPS (约40-45%)
  • INT8推理：接近H100的70-80%（推理对算力要求低，Ascend优势场景）
  • HBM带宽：HBM2e ~1.5TB/s vs H100 HBM3 3.35TB/s（存储带宽是最大瓶颈）

DeepSeek验证：2025年DeepSeek R1在Ascend集群上训练成功，这是第一个证明"非NVIDIA硬件能训练前沿模型"的公开案例。意义 > 数字本身。

局限：软件生态(CANN)远不如CUDA成熟，开发者适配成本高。大规模集群(>1000卡)稳定性未验证。价格约H100的50-60%但受制裁供应不稳。

KNOW 大基金一二三期 ▾

国家集成电路产业投资基金，中国政府推动半导体自主的核心财政工具。三期合计超¥6,800亿（~$950B）。

一期(2014, ¥1,387亿)：重点投制造(SMIC拿了65%+)和设计(紫光)。结果：SMIC从28nm→14nm量产。但紫光债务危机暴露了"撒钱"策略的局限。

二期(2019, ¥2,041亿)：转向设备和材料 — SMEE(光刻)、北方华创(刻蚀/薄膜)、沪硅(硅片)。应对升级的出口管制。

三期(2024, ¥3,440亿)：史上最大。重点：先进封装(绕过光刻限制的替代路径) + AI芯片 + EDA工具。信号：政府认为封装可能比光刻更快突破瓶颈。

腐败问题：一期/二期多名高管落马（丁文武等），实际拨付效率和投向偏离严重。三期加强了审计但独立验证仍缺失。投资者应将"大基金三期¥3,440亿"打五折估计实际有效投入。
