§0 Phase 0: 独立思考Independent Thinking (Before Search)

⚠ 诚实标注 Honesty Disclosure
本Phase 0标题为"搜索前独立思考"，但实际撰写时已读完两位代理人的完整MD文件（共40KB+）及CP2完整内容。这是事后重构，不是真正的搜索前独立思考。其价值在于：明确标记预判偏差方向，而非假装"猜对了"。

1. 节点解决什么问题？

存储/HBM解决"Memory Wall1"——计算与数据之间的带宽瓶颈。GPU算力指数增长但DRAM带宽增长滞后，HBM通过垂直堆叠12-16层DRAM+硅穿孔(TSV)互连，提供传统DDR 5-10倍带宽。如果HBM明天消失：所有AI训练/推理停摆，NVIDIA的B200/Rubin变废铁。

2. 供给约束？

  • HBM良率：垂直堆叠12-16层，TSV工艺2极难，HBM4新增逻辑层集成
  • 先进封装产能（CoWoS/MR-MUF3）是共享瓶颈
  • DRAM fab建设周期2-3年，产能不能快速响应
  • Die Penalty4: 1GB HBM消耗3-4倍标准DRAM晶圆面积 → AI吞噬传统DRAM供给

3. 需求驱动力？

  • AI模型规模指数增长 → 每代GPU配套HBM翻倍（H100: 80GB → B200: 192GB → Rubin5: 288-384GB）
  • 超大规模客户CapEx承诺锁定多年需求
  • 推理需求崛起 → 不再只是训练拉动
  • 自研ASIC芯片（Google TPU、AWS Trainium）同样需要HBM

4. 当前供需状态？

严重供不应求。2026年全部HBM产能已在2025年上半年预售完毕。三家供应商（SK Hynix+Samsung+Micron）只能满足核心客户50-66%需求。HBM3E涨价20%，客户接受。DRAM合约价2025年底同比+170%6。

5. 未来2-3年变化方向？

  • HBM4/HBM4E7技术迭代 → Samsung可能在新架构追平
  • 若AI投资放缓，HBM高beta属性将放大下行
  • 美国CHIPS Act政策给Micron额外支持（$6.2B直接资助）
  • 2027-2028新产能上线 → 供需拐点可能出现

KNOW Memory Wall ▾

The Memory Wall problem: CPU/GPU compute performance has been growing at ~50-60% CAGR while DRAM bandwidth grows at only ~20-25% CAGR. This widening gap means processors increasingly starve for data. HBM solves this by stacking 12-16 DRAM dies vertically with Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs), providing 5-10x the bandwidth of standard DDR5. For AI workloads, where models are too large to fit in on-chip memory, the memory bandwidth bottleneck is the primary constraint on throughput — not the number of compute units.

Quantified gap: NVIDIA B200 GPU has 11.2 TFLOPS of BF16 compute but needs 8TB/s of memory bandwidth to keep it fed. Standard DDR5 provides ~51.2 GB/s per channel. HBM3E provides 1.2TB/s per stack. Without HBM, the B200 would spend ~90% of its cycles waiting for data. This is why HBM is not optional — it is architecturally required for modern AI accelerators.

KNOW TSV工艺 ▾

Through-Silicon Vias (TSV): Vertical copper interconnects drilled through silicon dies, enabling chip stacking. For HBM, each stack has 12-16 DRAM dies + 1 logic die connected by thousands of TSVs. The manufacturing challenge: (1) Each via must be perfectly aligned across all layers (<1 micron tolerance), (2) thermal management across stacked dies, (3) yield drops exponentially with layer count — a single defective die kills the entire stack. This is why HBM yield is structurally lower than standard DRAM, and why "die penalty" of 3-4x exists.

HBM4 innovation: HBM4 integrates a logic layer (from TSMC or Samsung foundry) at the bottom of the stack, replacing the passive silicon interposer. This adds design complexity but enables higher bandwidth (>2.8TB/s) and lower power. SK Hynix's 11.7Gbps achievement at CES 2026 demonstrates this approach works.

KNOW CoWoS vs MR-MUF封装 ▾

CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate): TSMC's advanced packaging technology used to integrate GPU dies with HBM stacks on a silicon interposer. This is the primary bottleneck for AI chip production — NVIDIA's Blackwell and Rubin both require CoWoS. TSMC has been expanding CoWoS capacity aggressively but demand still outstrips supply.

MR-MUF (Mass Reflow Molded Underfill): Samsung's alternative to CoWoS, used for their HBM packaging. Potentially lower cost but historically had yield issues that delayed Samsung's HBM3E qualification. Samsung's HBM4 "highest NVIDIA rating" suggests MR-MUF issues may have been resolved.

Why it matters for this node: HBM supply is constrained at TWO levels: (1) DRAM die production (wafer fab) and (2) advanced packaging (CoWoS/MR-MUF). Even if all three memory companies produce enough DRAM dies, the packaging step creates an additional bottleneck. This is the "shared bottleneck" referenced in Phase 0.

KNOW Die Penalty (3-4x) ▾

The HBM die penalty: 1GB of HBM requires 3-4x more DRAM wafer area than 1GB of standard DDR5. This is because: (1) HBM uses wider I/O interfaces (1024-bit vs DDR5's 64-bit) requiring more silicon area per die, (2) TSV keep-out zones consume additional die area, (3) yield losses from stacking reduce effective output. TrendForce estimates AI workloads will consume ~20% of global DRAM wafer capacity in 2026 — but only produce a fraction of total DRAM bits.

Economic consequence: HBM "taxes" the rest of the memory market. As memory fabs shift capacity toward HBM (higher margin), traditional DRAM and NAND supply gets squeezed, causing prices to rise across ALL memory products. Fortune's 2026-03-19 article documents this: smartphone, PC, and automotive OEMs are facing memory cost inflation driven by AI's appetite for HBM. DRAM contract prices rose 170% YoY by end of 2025 — much of this is the HBM die penalty effect.

KNOW NVIDIA Rubin: HBM倍增 ▾

HBM per GPU generation: H100: 80GB HBM3 → B200: 192GB HBM3E → Vera Rubin: 288-384GB HBM4. Each generation roughly doubles the HBM requirement. This is the structural demand driver that both CEOs cite: even if the number of GPUs sold stays flat, HBM demand doubles per generation. Combined with expanding GPU volumes, total HBM demand follows an exponential curve.

Rubin NVL144: NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform (announced GTC 2026) integrates 7 chip types into 5-rack supercomputers with 3.6 EFLOPS of FP4 performance. Each NVL144 system requires massive HBM4 allocation. SK Hynix claims ~70% supply share for Rubin HBM4 (UBS estimate), while Samsung's "highest NVIDIA rating" for HBM4 suggests the actual allocation may be more competitive than UBS predicts.

KNOW DRAM价格飙升+170% ▾

DRAM contract price surge: By end of 2025, DRAM contract prices had risen ~170% year-over-year. This is far beyond normal cyclical upturns (~30-50% in typical upcycles). The drivers: (1) HBM die penalty shifting capacity away from standard DRAM, (2) AI server builds consuming unprecedented DRAM volumes, (3) supply growth constrained at 16% (IDC) vs demand growth of 130%+ for HBM.

Impact on traditional markets: The Fortune article (2026-03-19) describes this as "AI's memory tax on the economy." Smartphone OEMs face 40-60% higher memory BOM costs. PC manufacturers are delaying launches due to DRAM pricing. Automotive memory costs have doubled. This creates a potential political backlash risk: if consumer electronics prices rise visibly due to AI's memory hoarding, regulatory attention may follow.

KNOW HBM4 vs HBM4E路线图 ▾

HBM4 (2026): 12-16 layers, 36-48GB per stack, >2.8TB/s bandwidth per stack, 11-11.7Gbps I/O speed. Key innovation: integrated logic die at bottom of stack (from foundry, not memory fab). All three companies now in mass production as of early 2026.

HBM4E (2027): Expected to hit 40% of 2027 HBM market (TrendForce). Further bandwidth and density improvements. Samsung and SK Hynix both targeting H1 2026 completion of HBM4E development, with mass production in 2027. This is the next battlefield for market share.

Competitive implication: HBM4 is where Samsung may close the gap with SK Hynix. The logic die integration means foundry relationships (TSMC vs Samsung Foundry) become a new competitive variable. SK Hynix uses TSMC for its logic die; Samsung uses its own foundry. Micron has not disclosed its logic die partner but is likely working with TSMC.

KNOW M15X工厂详情 ▾

M15X specs: Located in Cheongju, South Korea. $15.1 billion investment — SK Hynix's largest single capital project ever. Opened October 2025 (ahead of schedule). Began 1b-node DRAM commercial production February 2026 (4 months ahead of plan). Initial capacity: ~10,000 wafer starts/month. Target: 55,000-60,000 wafers/month by end of 2026. Full capacity ~50,000 wafers/month by mid-2027.

Strategic significance: M15X is specifically designed for HBM4 and advanced DRAM production. The fact that it was built and ramped ahead of schedule is the strongest single evidence point for SK Hynix's execution capability. In semiconductor manufacturing, being 4 months early on a $15B fab is extraordinary — typical schedule slippage is 3-6 months late.

KNOW Micron纽约Megafab ▾

$100B NY Megafab: Located in Onondaga County, New York. Groundbreaking January 2026. Four fabs planned, creating 50,000 direct and indirect jobs. Largest private investment in New York State history. Largest semiconductor facility in US history. CHIPS Act provides $6.2 billion in direct federal subsidies.

Timeline: First fab operational around 2030. This is a 5-10 year strategic bet on AI memory demand being structural, not cyclical. The fact that Mehrotra committed to $100B with a 2030 timeline is the strongest evidence that he believes "this time IS different" for the memory cycle — you don't build a 4-fab megacomplex for a 3-year supercycle.

Geopolitical angle: Micron is positioning itself as the "American memory champion" in a semiconductor world increasingly shaped by US-China-Korea geopolitics. CHIPS Act support + Trump administration partnership creates a structural policy moat that Korean competitors cannot replicate in the US market.

KNOW 5年战略客户协议(SCA) ▾

What changed: Memory has traditionally been sold on quarterly or annual contracts with prices negotiated each cycle. Micron's first 5-year SCA (announced March 2026) is a structural break from this model. The customer (unnamed) committed to multi-year volume and pricing, transforming memory from a spot/quarterly commodity into a long-term strategic input. Micron is negotiating additional SCAs with other customers.

Why it matters: If SCAs become industry standard, the traditional "memory cycle" (build → oversupply → crash → cut production → shortage → build) could be partially dampened. Long-term contracts smooth both revenue and capital planning. This is the single strongest piece of evidence for the "structural break" thesis — and the newest, announced just days ago.

Skeptic's view: One SCA does not make a trend. The customer may have signed out of desperation during a shortage (buy insurance when scared), not out of structural demand conviction. If supply normalizes in 2027-2028, will new customers sign SCAs, or will they return to quarterly spot buying to get lower prices? The durability of SCAs will only be tested in a downcycle.
