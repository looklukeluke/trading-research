§3 Cognitive Biases / 认知偏差

English: While compiling this node, several cognitive biases were identified that could skew analysis.

中文：在编制本节点时，发现若干认知偏差可能会影响分析的客观性。

Selection Bias Warning

Over‑representing US‑centric platforms (Netflix, Disney+) while under‑representing emerging‑market SVOD services such as iflix (now part of Tencent) or ViX in Latin America.

Confirmation Bias Critical

Assuming “cord‑cutting” is an irreversible trend, ignoring evidence of cord‑re‑cutting in some demographics where pay‑TV subscriptions have stabilized.

Availability Heuristic Warning

Assigning disproportionate weight to the 2020‑2021 pandemic streaming surge as a permanent structural shift, rather than a short‑term spike.

Anchoring Bias Warning

Using early‑2010 subscriber numbers as a baseline for growth forecasts without adjusting for market saturation and platform diversification.

Survivorship Bias Critical

Focusing on successful platforms (Netflix, Spotify) while overlooking defunct or merged services (Vine, Yahoo Screen) that could provide corrective insights.

§4 Upstream & Downstream Cross / 上游下游交叉

English: This section maps inter‑dependencies between content production (upstream) and delivery infrastructure (downstream), highlighting feedback loops that affect both.

中文：本节映射内容生产（上游）与传输基础设施（下游）之间的相互依赖性，揭示影响双方的反馈回路。

Upstream → Downstream：
Content‑licensing deals determine the volume of high‑bitrate streams, directly influencing CDN bandwidth costs. For example, Netflix’s acquisition of Hollywood blockbusters raises average bitrate requirements, forcing CDNs to expand edge capacity.
Downstream → Upstream：
User‑engagement data (watch‑time, rewind rates) informs original‑content commissioning. Platforms such as Bilibili analyze 弹幕 (danmu) density to decide whether to fund a new anime series.
Regulatory Feedback：
Geo‑blocking policies upstream (e.g., China’s content quotas) shape downstream ISP throttling strategies to manage international traffic.

Lack of granular subscription‑tier pricing data for Southeast Asia, Africa, and Latin America hampers accurate revenue forecasting.
Live‑Sports Streaming Latency Metrics
Real‑time latency data for live sports events across various CDN providers is proprietary and rarely disclosed, making performance benchmarking difficult.
Platform‑Specific Engagement Metrics
Detailed watch‑time distribution (e.g., % of users finishing a series vs. dropping off after the first episode) is not publicly available for most platforms, especially in China.
Advertising Revenue Attribution for AVOD
Attribution models for ad‑supported streams (e.g., how many ads are skipped vs. viewed) are opaque, leading to uncertain ad‑ARPU estimates.

§7 Decision Log / 决策日志

English: Key decisions made during the construction of this node, with rationale.

中文：构建本节点时做出的关键决策及其理由。

Decision 1: Include Netflix in Global SVOD despite regional licensing restrictions.
Rationale: Netflix’s global footprint (190+ countries) outweighs the noise from geo‑restricted content, making it a primary benchmark for market sizing.
Decision 2: Exclude short‑form video platforms (TikTok, Douyin) from primary SVOD classification.
Rationale: Their consumption patterns (micro‑clips vs. long‑form) differ fundamentally; they are analyzed under the Live‑Streaming category for a more coherent node structure.
Decision 3: Use eMarketer 2025 AVOD growth figures as the primary source for ad‑supported revenue.
Rationale: eMarketer provides consistent methodology across regions, allowing cross‑country comparability; supplemented with App Annie’s platform‑level data for validation.
Decision 4: Treat China‑specific platforms (iQIYI, Bilibili) as a separate sub‑node due to regulatory and payment‑system differences.
Rationale: Separate treatment avoids conflating distinct market dynamics and allows targeted policy analysis.
