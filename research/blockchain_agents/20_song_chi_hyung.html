Song Chi-Hyung founded Dunamu in 2012, which operates Upbit — South Korea's dominant cryptocurrency exchange with an estimated 80% market share. Korea is the world's third-largest crypto market by volume per capita, and the "Kimchi premium" (price premium on Korean exchanges vs global markets) has been a persistent phenomenon. Upbit's dominance was built on a strategic partnership with Kakao, Korea's ubiquitous messaging platform.

Korea's Outsized Crypto Participation

An estimated 30%+ of South Korea's population actively trades crypto. This is dramatically higher than any other developed nation. Factors: high internet penetration, cultural affinity for speculation (real estate inaccessible for young Koreans), capital controls creating "walled garden" markets, and the Kakao-Upbit integration making access frictionless.

据估计韩国超过30%的人口活跃交易加密货币。这远高于其他发达国家。原因：高互联网普及率、投机文化（年轻人买不起房）、资本管制创造的"围墙花园"市场、以及Kakao-Upbit一体化使入场零摩擦。
