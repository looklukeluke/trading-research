01 Overview概要

C-tier rationale: Official Chinese government data — independently difficult to verify. Represents the "state-controlled digital currency" path, fundamentally opposed to decentralized crypto. Tracked as a geopolitical counterweight, not as a crypto ally.

Zhou Xiaochuan (former PBoC governor, 2002-2018) initiated the digital yuan research program. Mu Changchun (current head of PBoC Digital Currency Research Institute) leads the implementation. Together they architect e-CNY — the world's most advanced CBDC with 3.5 billion transactions and $2.4 trillion in circulation across 27 pilot cities. e-CNY includes smart contract capabilities and the cross-border mBridge experiment.

01 Action Evidence行动证据

e-CNY Pilot Program
Largest CBDC deployment globally. 27 cities, 3.5B transactions. Distribution via red packet campaigns, salary payments, and public transit integration. Adoption driven by government mandate rather than organic demand — a key distinction from decentralized crypto adoption.
2020-present · State deployment
mBridge Cross-Border CBDC
Joint project with BIS Innovation Hub, Hong Kong, Thailand, UAE central banks. Aims to create a SWIFT alternative for CBDC-to-CBDC settlements. Geopolitical significance: potentially enables sanctioned-nation trade settlement bypassing USD-dominated systems.
2022-present · Cross-border settlement
Comprehensive Crypto Ban
China banned crypto exchanges (2017), ICOs (2017), mining (2021), and all transactions (2021). This is the action evidence for the strategic choice: eliminate decentralized competitors to maximize e-CNY adoption and state monetary control.
2017-2021 · Regulatory action

04 Judgment判断

"Ban crypto, but build CBDC." The state-controlled digital currency path — fundamentally opposed to decentralized stablecoins. "禁止加密货币，但建设CBDC。" 国家控制的数字货币路线——与去中心化稳定币根本对立。

e-CNY and USDT serve completely different needs. e-CNY is state-controlled programmable money — it enables surveillance, conditional spending, and monetary policy enforcement. USDT/USDC are permissionless — they enable capital flight, sanctions evasion, and financial freedom. The two systems are not competitors; they are philosophical opposites serving different user bases.
